06 — Design Rationale

The Mark

Three strands braid upward and converge at a single point of light. Each strand represents a pillar of the organisation's work: the UK community, the Israeli charity HaMeshulash, and the at-risk youth it serves. Together — like the Biblical triple cord (Ecclesiastes 4:12) — they are not easily broken.

Colour Language

Gold evokes warmth, hope, and the history of the Jewish community. Deep Blue references the British sky and the Israeli flag. White stands for honesty, clarity, and the purity of the mission.

Typography

Cormorant Garamond brings the gravitas and warmth of established British philanthropic institutions. Inter provides modern clarity for navigation and body text — grounding the brand in contemporary digital practice.

Connection to HaMeshulash

The name Hut HaMeshulash (החוט המשולש) literally means "the triple strand". The logo is therefore a direct visual translation of the Israeli charity's own name — creating a seamless visual bridge between the UK donor community and the Israeli organisation on the ground.
